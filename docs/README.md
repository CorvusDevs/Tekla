Tekla is a floating virtual keyboard for macOS that lets you type by swiping across letters or tapping keys individually. Swipe a word, pick a smart prediction, and full sentences come together with minimal effort. Context-aware predictions, bigram language models, and 14 languages, all running 100% offline. A one-time purchase with a 14-day free trial.

## Features

- **Swipe-to-Type.** Slide your finger or pointer across letters to type words instantly, powered by geometric scoring and Bayesian ranking.
- **Smart predictions.** Context-aware word predictions using bigram language models and frequency data, with next-word suggestions.
- **14 languages.** Full keyboard layouts and prediction data for English, Spanish, German, French, Portuguese, Italian, and 8 more.
- **100% offline.** No internet required. All processing and language data stays entirely on your device.
- **Full desktop layout.** Complete Mac keyboard with function keys, modifier keys, and a navigation cluster in a resizable floating window.
- **Lightweight.** Near-zero CPU when idle, thanks to an event-driven architecture that only works when you do.

## Accessibility

Tekla is built for people who need an alternative to a physical keyboard:

- **Motor accessibility.** Form words with a single continuous gesture instead of pressing individual keys. Less movement, less strain.
- **Flexible input.** Works with trackpads, mice, styluses, head pointers, and any pointing device. Make the keys as large as you need.
- **Fewer keystrokes.** Smart predictions and next-word suggestions mean you type less to say more.

## Requirements

- macOS 14 Sonoma or later
- Apple Silicon or Intel Mac

## Download and install

1. Download the latest release from the [Releases page](https://github.com/CorvusDevs/Tekla/releases/latest) or the [website](https://corvusdevs.github.io/Tekla/).
2. Open the `.dmg` and drag Tekla into Applications.
3. Launch Tekla and grant Accessibility permission when prompted, so it can type into any app.

## Pricing

## Privacy

All processing happens locally on your device. No data is ever sent anywhere. No accounts, no analytics, no telemetry, no network requests.
